Problem Background

The urgency of the food loss and waste issue in Indonesia.

Financial & Ecological Losses

Indonesia ranks 44th out of 78 countries, wasting around 20.9 million tons of food annually. For F&B businesses, this is a sunk cost that hurts margins and contributes to carbon emissions.

Food Access Gap

Millions of people, including student and worker demographics, desperately need access to quality food at more affordable and economical prices.

Market Gap

There is no centralized digital platform in Indonesia that comprehensively integrates commercial solutions (selling surplus food at a discount) and social solutions (donations) into a single ecosystem.

Information System Solution

A Hybrid Model approach to bridge the market gap.

Commercial Module

B2B to B2C

A system enabling merchants (restaurants/cafes) to upload their expiring surplus food inventory in real-time.

  • Price discounts of 50-80%.
  • Primary target: Students & workers.

Charity Module

Social Donation

An integrated one-click donation system to help communities in need directly through the app.

  • SOS Food feature.
  • Distribution through partner NGOs.

Scope & Key Features

Technical specifications of the GigitAja Project Scope.

Smart Match Algorithm (AI)

Artificial intelligence to match surplus food supply with nearby user preferences and geolocation to accelerate sales turnaround time.

Merchant Dashboard

A dedicated module for food providers to monitor surplus inventory, set instant discounts, and view revenue analytics.

Integrated Payment System

Integration of Payment Gateways (e.g., E-Wallets & QRIS) to facilitate transparent, secure, and seamless transactions.

Gamification

A reward or point system for users (both buyers and donors) to increase app retention and long-term engagement.

SWOT Analysis

Strategic evaluation of the GigitAja platform.

Strengths

  • Charity + commercial model attracts a broad market segment.
  • Social and environmental focus enhances business image.
  • Extensive potential partner network (restaurants, MSMEs, etc.).
  • Affordable prices highly appeal to student and worker segments.

Weaknesses

  • Highly dependent on partners (restaurants, MSMEs) for food supply.
  • Difficult to consistently maintain surplus food quality.
  • Market needs education to normalize buying surplus food.
  • Charity model requires long-term funding to remain sustainable.

Opportunities

  • Increasing public awareness regarding food waste reduction.
  • Collaboration prospects with NGOs, startups, and the government.
  • Emerging regulatory support for green/sustainability businesses.
  • Digital technology developments ease rapid business expansion.
  • Potential collaboration with existing food delivery platforms.

Threats

  • Competition from NGOs and food banks with similar goals.
  • Large food delivery platforms could easily adopt this business model.
  • Challenges in logistics and distribution of surplus food.
  • Strict government regulations regarding food safety.
  • Unpredictable changes in public consumption habits.

Assumptions & Risk Management

Part 3 of the GigitAja Project Charter.

1. Business Competition Risk

Major players like GoFood/GrabFood releasing similar surplus food features.

Mitigation: Strong focus on the Hybrid Model (Charity + Commercial) and building niche market loyalty among university communities that is hard for large corporations to replicate.

2. Food Quality Risk

Incidents of spoiled or unfit food being sold/donated.

Mitigation: Strict Terms & Conditions (T&C) transferring food damage liability to Merchants, alongside a transparent rating & review system.

3. Technology Risk

Server downtime during simultaneous high traffic (e.g., right before restaurant closing times).

Mitigation: Utilizing an auto-scalable cloud hosting architecture.
